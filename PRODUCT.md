# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary audience: startup founders and prospective clients evaluating Hammad for freelance/contract full-stack engineering work. They're scanning quickly for proof of capability (real projects, measurable outcomes) and credibility (past clients, testimonials), then deciding whether to book an intro call. Secondary: recruiters/hiring managers doing the same evaluation for full-time roles.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that demonstrates full-stack engineering craft and track record, and converts visiting founders/clients into booked calls (via Calendly) or direct contact. The site itself is a work sample — its own execution quality is evidence of the skill being sold.

## Brand Personality

Bold, creative, memorable — voice is confident and results-driven (concrete numbers: "$10k to $500k/month," "reduced manual effort by 50%"), not corporate-safe or understated. The site should take visible design risks rather than blend into generic SaaS/template conventions.

## Anti-references

- Generic Bootstrap/Tailwind-template feel: predictable blue-gradient hero, identical icon+text card grids, default component shapes with no point of view.
- Corporate/dashboard-y sterility: enterprise SaaS admin-panel polish reads too utilitarian for a portfolio meant to feel bold and personal.

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't tell** — lead with concrete outcomes and real project evidence over adjectives; the existing resume-style copy with hard numbers is the right instinct, the visual system should match that same specificity.
2. **Craft is the pitch** — since the audience is evaluating hiring the person who built this site, execution quality (motion, typography, responsiveness, performance) is itself the strongest credibility signal.
3. **Booking is the conversion event** — Calendly/contact CTAs are the primary action throughout, not an afterthought in the footer.
4. **Confidence without cliché** — bold and memorable, but earn it structurally (distinct layout, type, motion) rather than through decoration alone; avoid the generic-template patterns called out above.
5. **Respect the visitor** — fast, accessible, and motion that enhances rather than gates content.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline: ≥4.5:1 body text contrast, ≥3:1 large text, full keyboard navigation, visible focus states. All motion/animation must respect `prefers-reduced-motion` with a crossfade/instant fallback, not just have it as decoration.
